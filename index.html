The Leveled-Reader Library provides instruction for each of the two leveled readers included in each unit in each and every grade. The lesson plans include presentation slides and writing prompts.
Student WorkText Course
Online Student Center
The Student WorkText Course pairs a printed consumable with digital resources to help you build context and a general understanding of the concepts outlined in the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards. Each of the units is governed by a Big Idea and the lessons all start with an Essential Question to help focus learning. Inquiry Extension projects are included in the WorkText to align the program more closely with the C3 Framework. Students will love writing in the consumables as well as interacting with the maps, games, and animations online in the Online Student Center.
Leveled-Reader Library Teacher Center
 
K – 5 Networks is the social studies program committed to helping teachers deliver quality instruction grounded in authentic documents and artifacts, focused on close reading and writing, critical thinking, and citizenship—all with minimal prep. The program is: •   Aligned to the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards •   Easy to use •   Focused on helping you develop strong readers and writers K–5 Networks brings together three pathways to instruction, giving teachers ultimate flexibility. The three pathways include the Student WorkText Course, Primary Source Library, and Leveled-Reader Library. Using the Teacher Implementation Guide, teachers have a bird's-eye view of the three pathways, and can choose which lessons they would like to plan to teach. The complete lesson plans are found online in ConnectED.

About K–5 Florida Networks
The Primary Source Library puts authentic artifacts at your fingertips and each lesson references the same Close Reading and writing routine: •   Read•   Re-read•   Integrate The sources are often paired to help spark conversations and inspire further investigation. The Document Detective's Notebook feature helps students craft their own probing questions about primary sources. Each lesson closes with a collection of writing prompts where students plan their responses using graphic organizers and develop their voices.
